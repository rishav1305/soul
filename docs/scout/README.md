## 2. Job Discovery Strategy

### The Problem

Most people limit themselves to 5-10 job boards. But:
- Many companies only post on their own career page (via Greenhouse, Lever, Workable) and never syndicate to LinkedIn/Indeed.
- 60-80% of senior roles are never publicly posted at all.
- Different platforms surface different opportunities — no single board has everything.

### The Solution: 3-Layer Discovery

```
Layer 1: TheirStack API (free tier — PRIMARY)
├── 321,000+ sources including LinkedIn, Indeed, Naukri, Glassdoor
├── 16,000+ ATS career pages (Greenhouse, Lever, Workable, Ashby)
├── 175,000+ company career sites
├── Auto-deduplication (same job on multiple boards → one entry)
├── Tech stack intelligence (who's using which AI stack)
├── Company enrichment (funding, employee count, industry, LinkedIn)
├── Hiring manager identification (name + LinkedIn URL)
├── 200 free API credits/month
└── Filter by: keywords, company size, funding stage, location, tech stack

Layer 2: Manual Platform Browsing (supplement TheirStack)
├── Wellfound — startup jobs (profile active ✓)
├── LinkedIn — with Premium, use advanced search + alerts
├── Naukri — with FastForward active, check recruiter messages
└── Community job channels — Discord/Slack (see Communities section)

Layer 3: Inbound (compounds over time)
├── LinkedIn recruiter DMs (from Premium visibility + content)
├── Naukri recruiter contacts (from FastForward visibility)
├── X/Twitter DMs — senior AI roles posted as tweets, filled via DMs
├── Community referrals from Discord/Slack participation
├── Wellfound — startups browse and reach out
├── Vetted networks (Hired, Toptal, Turing) — companies matched to you
└── Content-driven inbound (from weekly analysis posts)
```

#### Sweep Configuration


### Why This Filter Works

1. **Job titles are comprehensive** — covers every naming variation companies use for AI roles (36 titles). Companies call the same role different things — "LLM Engineer" at one company is "Applied Scientist" at another.

2. **Title exclusions filter noise** — removes intern/junior/fresher listings that waste credits and review time.

3. **Tech stack filtering is the killer feature** — companies using `anthropic`, `langchain`, `pytorch` in their stack will value your specific experience. TheirStack identifies these companies before the listings even go wide.

4. **Seniority filter** — ensures results match your 8+ years experience level.

5. **Geography is strategic** — India primary for in-person, but remote roles from US/EU/SG expand opportunities 10x.

6. **7-day window** — first 48 hours after posting has highest HM response rate. Weekly sweep catches everything fresh.

### TheirStack Data Enrichment (What You Get Per Lead)

Each TheirStack result auto-populates Scout leads with:

```
Job Details:     title, description, location, remote/hybrid, seniority
Company Intel:   name, domain, industry, employee count, LinkedIn URL
Funding Data:    total funding USD, funding stage
Hiring Manager:  full name, LinkedIn URL (when available)
Compensation:    salary string, min/max annual USD, currency
Tech Stack:      technology slugs used by company
Employment Type: full-time, contract, part-time → auto-routes to pipeline
Source:          original job board URL + company career page URL
```

This means every lead arrives pre-enriched. No manual research needed for Tier 2/3 applications. For Tier 1, the hiring manager LinkedIn URL enables direct warm outreach.

## 3. Job Application Strategy (AI-Driven Pipeline with Human Gates)

**Full spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-03-18-job-application-strategy-design.md`

### Core Principle

**Never cold-apply if you can warm-apply.** At senior level (8+ years), cold applying on portals puts you in the same pile as fresh graduates. TheirStack's value is intelligence — it tells you who is hiring before the crowd, giving you a window to get warm.

**AI proposes, you dispose.** Scout automates discovery, classification, scoring, resume tailoring, and outreach drafting. You only intervene at three human gates: morning batch review, follow-up approval, and stale lead decisions.

### 3.1: Tier Classification Engine

**Step 1 — Instant Classification (on discovery, zero API cost):**

| Tier | Rule | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** (warm-only) | Dream company list match OR funding > $50M OR employees > 500 in AI industry OR tech stack includes anthropic/openai/deepmind | Never apply cold. Outreach first. |
| **Tier 2** (career page + outreach) | Series A+ funded AND AI tech stack AND 50-500 employees | Apply on career page + LinkedIn HM outreach same day |
| **Tier 3** (speed apply) | Everything else that passed TheirStack filters | Apply on portal, speed matters |

**Dream company list** (`~/.soul-v2/dream-companies.json`, user-maintained):
Anthropic, DeepMind, OpenAI, Google AI, Meta AI (FAIR), Microsoft Research, Apple ML, Amazon AGI, Cohere, Mistral, Databricks, Hugging Face, Stability AI, Adept, Character.AI, Inflection, xAI, Perplexity, Runway, Midjourney

**Step 2 — Match Scoring (async, Claude via OAuth — Tier 1/2 only):**

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| > 85 | Promote tier (Tier 2 → Tier 1 treatment) |
| 70-85 | Keep current tier, proceed |
| < 70 | Demote or skip |

Tier 3 leads scored only if batch has spare capacity.

### 3.2: Pipeline Stages (Job Pipeline)

```
discovered → qualified → preparing → outreach-sent → applied →
screening → interview → offer → joined

Terminal: joined, rejected, withdrawn, skipped
```

| Stage | Meaning | Triggered By |
|---|---|---|
| `discovered` | Lead ingested. Tier auto-assigned. | Automated (sweep) |
| `qualified` | Match scored >70. Decision to pursue. | Automated (scoring) |
| `preparing` | AI generated tailored resume + cover letter. Ready for review. | Automated (AI tools) |
| `outreach-sent` | LinkedIn connection/message sent (Tier 1/2). Cadence starts. | Human (Gate 1) |
| `applied` | Application submitted on career page/portal/via referral. | Human |
| `screening` | Recruiter/HM responded. In active process. | Human |
| `interview` | Interview(s) scheduled or in progress. | Human |
| `offer` | Offer received. Negotiation phase. | Human |
| `joined` | Accepted and started. Terminal. | Human |
| `skipped` | Score <70 or manually decided not to pursue. Terminal. | Auto or Human |

**Stage skipping:**
- Tier 3: skip `outreach-sent` (discovered → qualified → preparing → applied)
- Referral path: can skip `preparing` (outreach-sent → applied via referrer)

### 3.3: Automated Flow with Human Gates

```
PHASE 1: DISCOVER (automated, daily)
  TheirStack sweep → leads ingested → tier auto-classified → duplicates filtered

PHASE 2: QUALIFY (automated, Tier 1/2)
  Claude resume_match scoring → score > 70 → "qualified" / score < 70 → "skipped"

PHASE 3: PREPARE (automated)
  Claude generates: tailored resume, cover letter, outreach draft, salary estimate
  Lead moves to "preparing"

  ★ GATE 1: MORNING REVIEW (you, ~30 min daily)
  Scout Actions tab shows batch. For each lead:
    Company, tier, score, resume diff, cover letter, outreach draft, salary, HM LinkedIn
    Actions: [Approve] [Edit] [Skip] [Change Tier]

PHASE 4: EXECUTE (you, per tier)
  Tier 1 → send LinkedIn outreach → "outreach-sent"
  Tier 2 → apply career page + LinkedIn HM outreach → "applied"
  Tier 3 → apply portal → "applied"

PHASE 5: FOLLOW-UP (automated cadence + human gates)
  Lead-attached outreach (Tier 1/2):
    Day 0:  Connection request sent
    Day 3:  Accepted? → ★ GATE 2: review follow-up draft
            Not accepted? → engage with their content
    Day 5:  Second touch (comment on their post, re-approach)
    Day 8:  No movement → ★ GATE 3: fall back to Tier 2 approach?
    Day 14: No response → auto-mark "withdrawn"

  Relationship outreach (not lead-attached):
    No cadence. No auto-demotion. Long game.
    Tracked in "referral" pipeline separately.
```

|---|---|

| Prepared by AI | 8-10 |

### 3.5: Where to Apply (Decision Matrix)

```
Lead qualified (score > 70)
  │
  ├─ Has referral? → Referrer submits internally. Do NOT apply on portal.
  │
  ├─ Tier 1 (no referral)? → Do NOT apply yet. Outreach first.
  │   → Day 8 fallback: apply on company career page
  │
  ├─ Tier 2? → Apply on company career page (same day)
  │   + LinkedIn outreach to HM (same day)
  │
  └─ Tier 3? → Apply on portal where found (speed matters)
      + Find CTO/VP Eng on LinkedIn if possible
```

**Career page discovery:** TheirStack provides `final_url` (career page) + `source_url` (portal). Priority: `final_url` > `source_url`.

**What to submit per tier:**

| Tier | Materials |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Tailored resume + cover letter (only after warm path exhausted) |
| Tier 2 | Tailored resume + cover letter + LinkedIn note to HM |
| Tier 3 | Tailored resume (cover letter optional) |

**What to avoid:**
- LinkedIn Easy Apply (3% response rate)
- Indeed/Glassdoor/Monster for senior AI roles (stale reposts)
- Generic resume (tailored gets 78% higher response)
- Applying on multiple portals for same company
- Applying before exhausting warm path for Tier 1

## 4. Networking & Referral Strategy (Multi-Intent, AI-Driven)

**Full spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-03-18-networking-strategy-design.md`

### Why Networking Is The Strategy

- 60-80% of senior roles are never posted on any job board
- 85% of positions are filled through referrals and networking
- Referred candidates are 15x more likely to be hired
- Success rate: 33-80% (networking) vs 4-10% (job boards)

Section 3 (Job Applications) handles the 20-40% that ARE posted. This section handles the 60-80% that aren't.

### Core Principle

**Lead with value, discover intent.** Never open with what you want. Build the relationship, understand their needs, then match your offering (job, freelance, contract, consulting, team) to their situation. The same connection could lead to any outcome — don't narrow it prematurely.

### 4.1: Contact Types & Intent

| Contact Type | Primary Intent | Ask Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring Manager (AI lab/startup) | Job | 7+ interactions (READY) |
| Founder / CTO (startup) | Freelance / Contract | 2-3 interactions (ACTIVE) |
| Peer Engineer (senior IC) | Referral + mutual | 4-5 interactions (WARM) |
| AI Researcher | Relationship | No direct ask — long game |
| VC / Investor | Intel | Never ask — they connect naturally |
| Community Leader | Visibility | Never ask — stay helpful |
| Dev Advocate | Amplification | Never ask — share good work |

### 4.2: Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Volume/week | Your Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Primary outbound — targeted, AI-drafted | 15-20 connects + 10-15 comments | 20 min review |
| **X/Twitter** | Engagement layer — strategic replies | 10-15 thoughtful replies | 10 min review |
| **Email** | Freelance/consulting cold outreach | 3-5 emails | 5 min review |
| **Communities** | Observe-first, your pace | No targets | When comfortable |

**LinkedIn:** AI drafts connection requests (never mention job intent), follow-up messages, and comment replies on targets' posts. Templates per contact type. Never use "great post!" — always add insight.

**X/Twitter:** AI monitors target people's tweets, drafts replies that add genuine value. After 3-4 public interactions, DMs become natural. Not cold outreach.

**Email:** AI researches target company, identifies specific gap, drafts "quick win" observation email. No pitch in email 1 — just value.

**Communities:** Weeks 1-2 read only. Weeks 3-4 answer questions. Week 5+ share insights. Never self-promote in general channels. Never cold DM without prior public interaction.

### 4.3: The Referral Journey — Warmth Scoring

| Level | Interactions | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| NEW | 0-1 | Just connected. Engage with their content. |
| ACTIVE | 2-3 | They know your name. Founder/CTO ask OK. |
| WARM | 4-6 | Real rapport. Coffee chat appropriate. Peer ask OK. |
| READY | 7+ | Genuine relationship. Dream company ask OK. |
| DORMANT | 30+ days no activity | AI surfaces re-engagement suggestions. |

**The ask is never scripted.** Context-dependent:
- "I saw [Company] is hiring for [X]. Is the team good?" (let them offer referral)
- "I'm exploring opportunities in this space" (they connect the dots)
- "I do [X] as a contractor — happy to chat if useful" (for founders)
- Sometimes they ask you first — match your offering to their needs

### 4.4: AI-Driven Networking Pipeline

```
PHASE 1: IDENTIFY (automated, weekly)
  TheirStack leads → extract HM + company contacts
  Dream companies → key people to connect with
  X/Twitter → researchers posting about relevant topics
  Inbound → profile viewers, post engagers

PHASE 2: GENERATE (automated, daily)
  AI drafts: connection requests, follow-ups, comments, X replies, emails
  All queued for review

  ★ GATE N1: NETWORKING REVIEW (you, 20 min, Tue/Thu)
  Scout shows batch: "8 LinkedIn drafts, 5 X replies, 2 emails"
  Actions: [Send] [Edit & Send] [Skip] [Snooze 1 week]

PHASE 3: TRACK (automated)
  Interaction count updated → warmth recalculated
  Dormant contacts surfaced for re-engagement

  ★ GATE N2: WEEKLY BRIEF (you, 10 min, Fri)
  "3 contacts → WARM (coffee chat?), 5 DORMANT (re-engage?), 2 READY (ask?)"

PHASE 4: CONVERT (human, when ready)
  AI suggests right ask per contact type + intent
  ★ GATE N3: You decide: ask, keep building, or not a fit
```

### 4.5: Weekly Throughput

**40-60 touchpoints/week, ~50 min of your time.**

Monthly contact growth: 42-63 new contacts across all types.

## 5. Freelance, Contract & Consulting Strategy

Freelance is a different game from job search. You're not applying — you're being found + doing outreach + writing proposals.

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)

Create profiles on all platforms listed in Section 1. Niche down: "I build [specific thing] for [specific client type]."

### Phase 2: Outbound Execution (Ongoing)

```
Strategy allocation:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Cold outreach (personalized)    40%   ← Highest ROI
Platform proposals (Upwork)     25%   ← Build reviews early
LinkedIn content + DMs          20%   ← Build authority
Inbound/referrals              15%   ← Grows over time
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### Cold Outreach That Works

1. Research a company → find a specific gap (bad website, no mobile app, slow loading, no AI features)
2. Send personalized email with a "quick win" observation
3. Don't pitch — offer value first
4. Follow up once after 5 days, then move on

### Platform Proposals (Upwork/Freelancer)

- **First 5 projects**: underprice slightly to build reviews. Reviews = everything on platforms.
- **Niche your profile**: "AI/LLM Engineer for enterprise applications" beats "Full Stack Developer"
- **Personalize every proposal**: Reference the client's specific project, don't use templates
- Apify Upwork scraper helps you find gigs early (before 50+ proposals pile up)

### Consulting (GLG/Guidepoint/Expert Collective)

- Sign up and pass their expert screening
- Expert network calls: ₹10-30K/hr for AI consulting
- Low time commitment, high rate, builds your consulting reputation
- These calls often lead to longer engagements

### Team/Company Contracts

| Action | Platform |
|---|---|
| List company | Clutch.co, GoodFirms |
| LinkedIn Company page | Credibility builder |
| Direct outreach to startups | Wellfound, LinkedIn — "We can be your dev team" |

### Pipeline Tracking in Scout

```
Freelance:  found → proposal-sent → shortlisted → awarded → delivering → completed
Contract:   discovered → applied → screening → interview → offer → engaged → completed
Consulting: lead → discovery-call → proposal-sent → negotiating → engaged → delivered
```

## 6. Social Media & Content Strategy

### Platform Priority

| Platform | Role | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Primary — recruiters, HMs, enterprise clients | 3 posts/week |
| **X/Twitter** | Secondary — researchers, AI labs, builders | 2-3 posts/week |
| **Substack** | Long-term — thought leadership, SEO, portfolio | 1 deep post/month |
| **GitHub** | Proof layer — demos, notebooks, code | Always linked |

**Skip:** Instagram, YouTube, TikTok — wrong audience, wrong ROI.

### Content Model: Weekly Analysis → 3-Part Series

One analysis per week, split into 3 standalone posts. You do the work once, distribute three ways.

```
Sunday   → Pick this week's analysis topic (from ongoing work)
Tuesday  → Part 1: The Hook Post
Thursday → Part 2: The Deep Dive
Saturday → Part 3: The Takeaway/Opinion
```

#### Part 1 — Hook Post (Tuesday)

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Create curiosity. Drive follows.
**Format:** Short, punchy. 100-150 words on LinkedIn. Single tweet on X.

```
Structure:
  Provocative finding or counterintuitive number
  → 1-line context (what you analyzed, why)
  → 2-3 bullet teaser of findings
  → "Breaking this down across 3 posts this week."
```

#### Part 2 — Deep Dive (Thursday)

**Purpose:** Deliver substance. Build credibility.
**Format:** Long LinkedIn post (300-500 words) + carousel or chart. Full thread on X.

```
Structure:
  Methodology (2-3 lines, shows rigor)
  → Key findings (3-5, with data/examples)
  → What surprised you
  → "Tomorrow: what this means and what I'd do differently"
```

Charts, even rough matplotlib outputs, work well here. Raw = authentic.

#### Part 3 — Opinion/Takeaway (Saturday)

**Purpose:** Convert readers into followers and leads. Most shareable post.
**Format:** Medium LinkedIn post (200-300 words). Punchy thread on X.

```
Structure:
  "After [analysis], here's my honest take:"
  → 3-5 actionable conclusions
  → One contrarian opinion (most shareable)
  → Open question to audience (drives comments → algorithm boost)
```

The open question is critical — comments in first 60 minutes = biggest LinkedIn algorithm signal.

### Content Pillars

| Pillar | % | What |
|---|---|---|
| **Builder Insights** | 40% | Real lessons from building your projects. War stories, architecture decisions, production failures. Most differentiated content. |
| **Curated Technical Takes** | 25% | React to papers, model releases, AI news with your own sharp angle. Don't summarize — evaluate. |
| **Career/Consulting Lessons** | 20% | Senior practitioner perspective. Project scoping, enterprise AI lessons, business judgment. Drives consulting inbound. |
| **Personal/Process** | 15% | Makes you human. Hardware builds, working from India targeting global labs, weekly updates. People hire people. |

### Analysis Types to Run

**Tier 1 — Highest impact (start here):**
- LLM benchmark deconstructions (re-run with production-style prompts)
- Cost/performance analysis (model comparisons at scale with real numbers)
- Failure pattern analysis (how production AI systems actually break)
- Insights derived from your own project usage data

**Tier 2 — Consulting inbound:**
- Vendor landscape maps ("The LLM evaluation tools landscape")
- Build vs buy analysis
- ROI frameworks for AI deployments

**Tier 3 — Community engagement:**
- Paper reproduction with your own commentary
- Public dataset analysis with an interesting finding
- Trend tracking (e.g., "AI Engineer job postings over 4 weeks")

### Cross-Platform Adaptation

Same analysis, different packaging:

```
LinkedIn Part 1 → Hook post (broad audience)
LinkedIn Part 2 → Deep dive with carousel (max reach)
LinkedIn Part 3 → Opinion post (engagement driver)

X Part 1        → Single punchy tweet
X Part 2        → Full technical thread (researchers love this)
X Part 3        → Hot take tweet (most RT-able)

Monthly         → Best-performing analysis → Substack deep-dive
GitHub          → Notebook/code if reproducible (massive credibility signal)
```

### Content Formats by ROI

| Format | ROI | Effort | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Text posts (LinkedIn + X) | Highest | Low | 3-4x/week |
| Carousels (LinkedIn PDF) | Very high (3-5x more impressions) | Medium | 1x/week |
| X threads | High for researchers | Low-Medium | 2x/week |
| Short video (LinkedIn native, 60-90s) | High (algo-favored) | Medium | 1x every 2 weeks |
| Substack blog | Long-term compounding | High | 1x/month |
| Images/infographics | Low standalone | Low | Supporting role only |

### Hook Writing Formulas

The first line determines everything. LinkedIn shows only 2 lines before "see more."

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Counterintuitive claim | "More context doesn't make LLMs smarter. It makes them lazier." |
| Specific number | "5,000 queries. 3 months. Here's what the data actually showed." |
| Hard-won lesson | "I made a ₹40L infrastructure mistake so you don't have to." |
| Provocative question | "Is your RAG pipeline actually retrieval-augmented — or just expensive search?" |
| Confession | "I over-engineered this for 6 months before I understood what users needed." |

### Key Rule

**"Good enough to publish beats perfect and unpublished."**

Your analysis doesn't need to be peer-reviewed. It needs to be honest, specific, and based on real work. Start with analysis you're already doing — don't create new work just for content.

## Summary: The System

```
DISCOVER (TheirStack + Apify + Communities)
    → QUALIFY (Claude tier_classifier + resume_match)
    → APPLY (company career page, tailored resume)
    → NETWORK (LinkedIn warm outreach, referrals)
    → BUILD VISIBILITY (3-part content series, community participation)
    → TRACK (Scout pipelines — job, freelance, contract, referral, inbound)
    → REVIEW (weekly metrics, adjust strategy)
    → COMPOUND (content → inbound → more opportunities)
```

**The key insight:** 5 warm applications beat 50 cold ones. Consistency over volume. The closed market is won by visibility + relationships, not applications. Lead with your work, not your resume.
